# Avrah Brand Design Brief

## Who is this for

We're looking for an illustrator, designer, or brand consultant to help us build the visual identity for Avrah. This document is meant to give you the full picture of who we are, what we care about, and how we want to feel to the world. We're not handing you a spec. We're giving you context and trusting you to interpret it.

## The company

**Avrah** (avrah.studio) is a product design studio. We take physical and digital products from early concept through to production. Our work spans firmware, electronics, IoT, cloud systems, web platforms, and data. We have deep engineering capability, but we are not just an engineering shop. We care about the people who use the things we build, and we believe the best technical work starts with understanding the human problem first.

We are small and intentional. The founder works directly with every client. When we need specialist depth (PCB layout, mechanical design, mobile development), we bring in trusted collaborators. We don't scale by hiring. We scale by choosing the right projects and doing them well.

## The name

Avrah comes from the Aramaic phrase *avra k'davra*, which means "I create as I speak." It's the origin of the word *abracadabra*. The idea that articulating something is the first step to making it real.

We liked that. A client describes a problem. A conversation turns into an architecture. An architecture turns into a product in someone's hands. That transformation is what we do. The name should carry that feeling. Not mystical. Not showy. Just the quiet confidence of people who make things exist.

## What we do (plain language)

- A healthcare company needs a device that connects to medical instruments over Bluetooth, sends patient data to the cloud, and works reliably in 65,000+ homes. We build that.
- Transit agencies across North America need to understand the condition of their rail networks. We build the sensors, the data pipelines, and the dashboards they use every day.
- A startup has an idea for a connected product but no engineering team. We figure out the architecture, build the prototype, get it through manufacturing, and hand them a product they can sell.

We work at the intersection of hardware and software. Most of our products have a physical thing in the world talking to a system in the cloud talking to a person through a screen.

## Who we work with

Early-stage startups who need a technical co-founder without giving up equity. Mid-size companies building their first connected product. Established organizations that need specialized embedded or IoT expertise they don't have in-house.

Our clients are usually technical enough to know what they want but need a team that can actually deliver it. They've often been burned by agencies that over-promised and under-delivered. They value directness and competence over polish and process theater.

## How we want to feel

**Playful, not frivolous.** We build serious systems, but we don't take ourselves too seriously. The brand should have warmth and personality. A little bit of magic. The kind of studio where the engineering is rigorous but there's a small illustrated character on the 404 page.

**Confident, not loud.** We don't need to convince anyone we're smart. The work speaks. The brand should feel like someone who's comfortable in the room without needing to dominate it.

**Precise, not cold.** We care about details. Tolerances, edge cases, the last 5% that makes something production-ready. But we also care about the person holding the device, the nurse reading the screen, the maintenance worker checking the dashboard at 6am.

**Human, not corporate.** We are real people. The founder answers emails. We say "we don't know" when we don't know. We'd rather lose a deal than over-promise. The brand should feel like talking to a person, not a company.

**Whimsical, not childish.** Think Studio Ghibli, not Cartoon Network. There's a difference between cute and immature. We want illustrations that make someone smile and then realize "oh, these people clearly know what they're doing." The contrast between playful visuals and deep technical work is the whole point.

**Physical and digital.** Our work lives in both worlds. Circuit boards and codebases. Sensor hardware and web dashboards. The brand should feel comfortable in both. Not purely techy, not purely industrial. Something in between.

## What we are not

- We are not a dev shop or agency. Don't make us look like one.
- We are not a startup. We don't move fast and break things. We move deliberately and ship things that work.
- We are not a big consultancy. No suits, no decks full of frameworks, no billable hours theater.
- We are not precious about aesthetics for their own sake. Design serves the work.
- We are not sterile. If the brand doesn't make someone smile at least once, something went wrong.

## Visual direction (open, not prescriptive)

We don't want to over-constrain this. Some starting points to react to, push back on, or build from:

**Color.** The current site uses a dark palette with a warm gold accent (#c9a55a). That was a placeholder decision but it feels close. The gold carries some of the Aramaic/ancient weight without being literal about it. Open to exploration but we probably live in a restrained palette. Not startup neons. Not corporate navy.

**Typography.** Clean, functional. We currently use Inter and JetBrains Mono. Something that works at small sizes on a spec sheet and at large sizes on a landing page. Personality through restraint, not through decorative type.

**Mark/logo.** We need something that works at favicon size, on a business card, on the side of a device enclosure if it ever came to that. The name AVRAH has strong letterforms. Could be a wordmark. Could be a monogram. Could be something abstract. We're open. It should feel solid enough to etch into metal but have enough personality to sit next to a cute illustration and not feel out of place.

**Imagery/illustration style.** This is the heart of what we're looking for. We want a cute, warm, digital illustration style that makes technical things feel approachable. Think of it this way: most IoT and embedded engineering studios look like they're selling to other engineers. We want to look like we're made by engineers who remember that real people use the things we build.

Some illustration concepts we're excited about:

- **Tiny worlds.** Small illustrated scenes for each thing we do. A neighborhood block with cute little parking sensors poking out of the ground. A cozy hospital room with a friendly device on the nightstand, little Bluetooth waves floating to a monitor. A train rolling along with sensor data radiating happily off the tracks. Each one is a contained little world that tells a story about our work.

- **The transformation.** Our tagline cycles through "concept to prototype, prototype to production, production to scale." There's an illustration series in there. The same object drawn three ways: a napkin sketch with coffee stain rings, a messy breadboard with wires everywhere, and then a clean finished product in someone's hand. The journey from idea to real thing, drawn with character.

- **Hands making things.** Hands sketching on paper. Hands soldering a board. Hands holding a finished device. Hands on a keyboard. We're a studio of people who make things. Illustrated hands give it personality without needing to draw faces or characters.

- **The invisible layer.** A cute device with its insides shown in cross-section. Little illustrated Bluetooth signals, tiny locks for encryption, small clouds with data flowing up to them. The thing we do is build everything you can't see. Make it visible, make it friendly.

- **The spark.** A small recurring motif tied to the "avra k'davra" creation idea. Could be a little spark, a tiny star, a subtle glow. Appears throughout the brand wherever something is being created or transformed. Not a mascot, just a recurring touch.

We don't need all of these. We want to see what an illustrator does with this territory. The right person will probably come back with something better than what we've described here.

## Deliverables (negotiable)

What we think we need, open to your recommendation on what actually matters:

- Logo/wordmark (with variations for light/dark, small/large)
- Color palette (current gold + dark is a starting point, but the palette should support warm, illustrated content)
- Typography system
- Illustration system: a set of spot illustrations for the website (capabilities, process, case studies) plus guidelines for how to create more in the same style
- Basic brand guidelines (enough to keep things consistent, not a 60-page brand book)
- Direction on how the website visual design should evolve to incorporate illustrations
- A 404 page illustration (seriously)

## Reference points (vibes, not instructions)

Studios and brands whose feel resonates, for different reasons:

- **Teenage Engineering** - playful precision, design-led but deeply technical
- **Studio Ghibli** - warmth, craft, attention to small details, cute without being childish
- **Kurzgesagt** - makes complex science feel friendly through illustration, bold colors, cute characters explaining serious things
- **Panic (Playdate)** - a serious tech company with a playful soul, proves you can be whimsical and credible
- **Linear** - clean, dark, purposeful. We like the restraint but want more personality on top
- **Field Notes** - small brand, strong identity, feels handmade but precise

These are not targets to copy. They're reference points. The sweet spot is somewhere between the warmth of Ghibli, the explanatory charm of Kurzgesagt, and the technical credibility of Linear. Cute and capable.
