Context

Existing solutions don't survive the street

In-ground sensors fail within 18 months. Camera systems require expensive infrastructure. Most cities that try smart parking abandon it within 3 years.


The problem isn't sensing technology. It's building hardware that survives snowplows, road salt, flooding, and 10 years of urban abuse while staying connected and accurate.

Key failures

  • Battery life measured in months, not years
  • Cellular connectivity gaps in urban canyons
  • No path from pilot to city-wide deployment
  • Data but no actionable enforcement workflow

Case Study

City of Winnipeg — Osborne Village Pilot

High-traffic commercial district with aggressive winters. Previous in-ground sensors failed after one freeze-thaw cycle. City needed a system that could survive the climate and provide real enforcement data.

We deployed 120 surface-mount sensors across 6 blocks in November 2025. Full mesh coverage using 4 solar gateways on existing streetlight poles.

Embedded IoT Cloud Dashboard

Results

  • Zero sensor failures through -40°C winter
  • Enforcement response time cut by 60%
  • $180K additional parking revenue projected annually
  • City approved Phase 2: 800 additional sensors
