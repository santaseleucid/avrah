About

Avrah

Aramaic
v. to create, to build

In ancient Aramaic, avra k’davra means “I create as I speak.” It’s where abracadabra comes from. The idea that saying something can make it real.

We took the first word. Avrah runs on two tracks: contract work that pays the bills and original ideas we develop into real products. Some become spin-offs. Some stay sketches. The point is to keep making things and see what sticks.

Team

Small on purpose.

A small core of people who make things. Engineers, illustrators, builders. When a project needs specialist depth, we bring in people we’ve worked with before.

No account managers, no layers between you and the work.

§02.1

RailView project

“See your rail network clearly.”

fig. 1 — system overview

What

Data management dashboard and grinding-program visualization for railway maintenance. Built under contract with REDACTED. Map-based views, trend analysis, and reporting for transit agencies across North America.

Why

Rail networks generate enormous amounts of condition data. RailView gives maintenance teams the tools to actually use it — prioritize work, visualize grinding programs, and catch problems before they become failures.

§02.2

RailSense project

Continuous rail defect monitoring from revenue service vehicles.

fig. 2 — system overview

What

Undercar noise and vibration sensors for transit railways. Custom hardware, AI-assisted maintenance prioritization. Every revenue service vehicle becomes a continuous track inspector.

Why

Dedicated inspection vehicles are expensive and run on schedules. RailSense monitors track condition during normal passenger service — continuous data, no operational disruption, problems caught earlier.

§02.3

OpenSpot project

“Know what’s available.”

fig. 3 — OpenSpot title card

What

Two-sided smart parking. Real-time occupancy for lot operators, spot-finding and reservations for drivers. Magnetometer or camera-based sensing, a dashboard for operators, and an app for everyone else.

Why

Parking is an infrastructure problem dressed up as a convenience problem. The wedge is municipal, hospital, and private lots — places where better data means real money saved and fewer cars circling blocks.

§03.1

Cyberdeck idea

“A computer that feels like it belongs to you.”

fig. 4 — form factor concept

What

A handheld computing device as aesthetic statement. Real keys, local AI, off-grid friendly. Open hardware, retro-futurist design, beautiful and useful in equal measure. Something you want to pick up.

Why

Consumer electronics are disposable black rectangles. A cyberdeck is the opposite — personal, repairable, opinionated. It’s a brand object that embodies everything Avrah believes about technology and craft.

§03.2

Community LoRa idea

“Off-grid, open, no subscription.”

fig. 5 — mesh topology

What

Community-forward open-source LoRa and mesh networking. Small, useful devices — a dog tracker, a weather station, a backcountry beacon. Built on Meshcore, designed to be shared and modified.

Why

Long-range radio is too useful to be locked behind subscriptions and proprietary ecosystems. Community LoRa puts mesh networking in people’s hands with open firmware, open hardware, and no monthly fee.

§03.3

Findables idea

“Where did I put that?”

fig. 6 — tag + localization

What

Cheap BLE tags with room-level indoor localization for your home and workshop. Open firmware, Home Assistant integration, no subscription. A 10-tag starter kit for under $130.

Why

AirTags are great for keys. But makers, hobbyists, and anyone with a workshop full of tools need something different — cheap enough to tag everything, open enough to plug into the systems you already run.
